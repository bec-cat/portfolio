Role: Visual Design, Hardware Research, Prototyping

Timeline: 12 weeks (Feb-May 2021)

Tools: Arduino IDE and Tinkercad

Overview

The final prototype is a walking stick with an ultrasonic sensor to assist object detection, and an accelerometer to function as a pedometer, using Arduino uno together with an app made with the help of open source code.

This project was created as part of prototyping physical interaction course in a group of 10 people; 10 researchers, 5 programmers, 5 product designs, 3 user testings. The design brief for this project involves physical game such as activity and movement.

Aims

The aim was to create a prototype device to assist vision impaired individuals with navigating to new places and encouraging physcial exercise through the use of a built in pedometer.

The main objectives:

  • Ascertain feasibility of prototype design for everyday use.
  • Test usability of proposed design and solution.
  • Determine if product assisted in improving the person's object detection.
  • Determine if the user finds the game/activity enjoyable.

Concept Ideation

To better conceptualize our prototype and expand upon idea, we decided to individually create our own version of our design. This allowed each team member to share their vision on how they believe the device should function and allowed us to generate more ideas to incorporate into the project. This concept ideation was essential to our process as one of the proposed designs was chosen to become our final project prototype.

Development & Design

As we had a better understanding of our problem space and prototype requirements, we focused our efforts on creating the final design. We split our group members into design/development teams to allow us to rapidly bring our idea to life. The development team worked on code while the design team worked on obtaining the necessary components for the physical design.
